It's right for you if...

You're looking to tackle huge days on huge mountains, whether it's an enduro race or an epic adventure. You want the light weight and muted vibes of a carbon frame, and you prioritize tunable suspension performance over a shiny top-shelf drivetrain.

The tech you get

A full OCLV Mountain Carbon frame with 160mm of travel and an all-new RockShox Super Deluxe Ultimate shock with an updated Thru Shaft damper, plus an all-new RockShox ZEB fork with extra-beefy 38mm stanchions. A Shimano XT 12-speed drivetrain for super-smooth shifting that's easy on the thumb, 4-piston brakes for confident descents, and Line Elite 30 carbon wheels with a 108-tooth Rapid Drive hub for quick acceleration when you need it most.

The final word

This is a beast of a mountain bike. You get hot new suspension technology normally reserved for the most expensive bikes, and you get it on a fresh new frame. With Slash 9.8, the faster you go, the better it rides, so giddy-up.

The Walkthrough

favorite_borderWhy you'll love it

  1. The RockShox ZEB fork's bigger 38mm stanchions help you stay on your line through the chunky stuff, while the updated damper lets you dial in the feel
  2. New, removable Knock Block has a bigger steering angle to keep cables and hoses safe without sacrificing turning radius
  3. We added a second down tube guard to better protect your frame from shuttle-related damage
  4. Wider seat tube, wider dropper—the 34.9mm diameter post is stronger, more durable, and moves up and down faster
  5. A large internal storage compartment in the down tube lets you stow tools and gear

Might as well be called Session Mini

Submitted 1 year ago

By Syngltrkmnd

From Bellingham, WA

I'm 44 and got my first mountain bike in the late 80s. I live in the Northwest where approaches are steep and sustained, and the gravity-fueled parts of the ride are steeper still. This is the only bike in my garage. I'm approaching two dozen rides on the '21 Slash after three fun seasons on the '18 Slash 9.7 (and a Remedy 9.8 29 before that) and I am really, REALLY impressed. Straight away, the updated geo on my XL frame is phenomenal. Paired with the new Super Deluxe rear shock, I feel a lot more centered and confident on the bike, particularly when descending. In addition the shock is much more active and tune-able; I'm adding a token to each the positive and negative air chambers for summer riding when velocities are a little higher than in the wetter seasons. The in-frame storage is genius - like a smartphone, how will we ever live without this? - and the frame protection is well thought-out. Lastly, I love the clear-coated 'windows' that reveal the gorgeous deep metallic cherry carbon weave. There's no terrain that seems to spook or unsettle this bike. This Slash probably isn't for everyone, but it's absolutely perfect for me. PS Big shout out to Trek Bikes Bellingham for outstanding service and support!

Steep, fast, and hairy. Pick three.

5

Great bike with very few trade-offs!

Submitted 2 years ago

By Bryceepoo

From Issaquah, WA

Describe Yourself 40+ Male hack

I was lucky to find one of these when they first came out, and I haven't been disappointed with my purchase at all. I ride 3-5 times a week at local trail networks like Duthie Hill Park, Tokul, Raging River and Tiger Mountain - so, a mix of big technical trails and bike skills park. The bike is light, stiff, engaging, and fast. The rear end is a highlight of the bike for me. The shock tune is outstanding with tons of flexibility (lockout, dampening switch). I LOVE the frame storage, I'm surprised how much I've enjoyed that. When I can get by with 1 water bottle, I have all my tools I used to carry in the frame and don't even need a pack. I've made a few changes to the bike, but mostly personal preference or specific to my area. Changed grips, seat, tires (btw, the Bontrager tires are actually really great, I'll run the stock set up next summer when it dries out here, but right now I have a SE5 in the rear and something with softer rubber than Trek offers in the front for the rain/roots here in the winter). Also, the wheels have been great. The hub engagement is super quick and isn't too loud. Like others have mentioned, if I had to pick on anything, it would be the spec on the SLX brakes, but it's hard to fault Trek for that. If you do a little research, the SLX brakes are functionally the same as XT's, just with less lever adjustability. Still, at $6k, and with a "XT" build package, I wish it included XT brakes. That said, they're powerful brake components and offer a good value and the rest of the XT build is great. Anyhow, I think Slash is a great bike for those looking to rip steep, aggressive tracks with speed and precision. Get one!
